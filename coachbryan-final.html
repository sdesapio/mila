The Approach

Creativity and intentionality.

Most coaches struggle with being fun and serious at the same time. Bryan doesn't. He holds both — and everything in between — without losing either.

He doesn't block out ideas from his students or turn down a thought. He lets you feel the music. He lets you be you. And when he wants to introduce something new, he asks — never tells.

"He does not block out ideas from his students. He lets you feel the music — and lets you be you."

This philosophy was shaped by years as a Disney On Ice performer and director, where Bryan learned that the most unforgettable performances come from genuine feeling — not perfect execution alone.

01

Your ideas belong in the program

When Bryan wants to suggest something, he asks: "What if you think about doing this?" — an invitation, never an instruction. Every idea he brings fits the skater's exact vision.

02

Genuine joy — not a coaching technique

When a skater lands something, Bryan is actually happy. Not performing enthusiasm — actually happy. Skaters feel the difference from the first session, and it changes everything.

03

Artistry and technique are one

Technical precision without expression is just an athletic exercise. Bryan's Disney On Ice background means he teaches both simultaneously — because audiences and judges feel the whole package.

04

No day but today

Bryan's guiding philosophy. Every session is its own complete thing — present, purposeful, and fully committed. No going through the motions.

The Coach

Performer. Director. Now yours.

Bryan Santiago joined Disney On Ice in 2011 as a Principal Performer — spending years bringing characters to life in front of audiences across the globe. In 2016, he became Performance Director, leading and developing international casts of professional skaters.

What he discovered in that role changed his path: developing talent is more fulfilling than performing. Watching someone find their voice on the ice — that became the thing.

"No day but today."— The philosophy Bryan coaches by

Now based at The Crossover Ice House in Leander, Bryan brings that world-class standard to the greater Austin area — coaching skaters of all levels with the same rigor he applied to international professionals.
